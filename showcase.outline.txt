# The Language
Plain text in, a finished deck out — this file is its own tutorial.

## Paragraphs and emphasis @2m
The first line of a slide is its lead — the room reads it before you speak.
Later lines are body paragraphs. Inline you get **bold**, *italic*, `code`,
mathematics like $e^{i\pi}+1=0$, and citations such as [@knuth84].
> Speaker notes live on N and feed the ?handout print.

## Steps reveal on the arrow key
- A plain bullet is always visible.
+ A `+` bullet appears when you press →.
++ A `++` bullet appears, then dims once you move past it.
+ Two leading spaces nest a point under the previous one.

## Held in reserve
Slides after a `::backup` section sit outside the count and the plan.

- PgDn still crosses over — the escape hatch is the point.
